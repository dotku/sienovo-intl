#CROSSLINK Series
#CROSSLINK vs XO3L

![](https://pub-048dcb96257f476697b113fcb5939cb9.r2.dev/blog/129233232/01_a4909fa97b6a0b28ca5ccf7e633936be.png)

Overall, XO3L offers greater flexibility and is more akin to a general-purpose CPLD; CROSSLINK is more specialized.

For the requirement of converting subLVDS to MIPI, CROSSLINK has an advantage due to its higher integration, leading to greater stability.

#Key Points
#CrossLink does not support JTAG download,
requiring a separate download cable.

![](https://pub-048dcb96257f476697b113fcb5939cb9.r2.dev/blog/129233232/02_0799b7371c87d8a0a255f79646d42b41.png)

#IO Power-up Voltage

![](https://pub-048dcb96257f476697b113fcb5939cb9.r2.dev/blog/129233232/03_83f7a7e242371a49e175cebc12c687bd.png)

#NVCM One-Time Programming (OTP)

![](https://pub-048dcb96257f476697b113fcb5939cb9.r2.dev/blog/129233232/04_0686d383a32a3cea5eda2b1056b9ba67.png)
If this one opportunity is used to configure the device to boot primarily from NVCM, subsequent operations will be limited to on-line debugging, and modifications to IO configurations will no longer take effect.
