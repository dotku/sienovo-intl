An engraving machine consists of three main parts: a CNC system, an engraving path calculation system, and a mechanical drive system [20]. The engraving path calculation system typically uses CAD (Computer-Aided Design) software such as AutoCAD, UGS, Pro/Engineer, etc., to convert the designed engraving product into engraving tool paths and store them in CNC files. The CNC system then parses the data in the CNC files, analyzes and calculates the corresponding data to convert it into motor speed, pulses, and corresponding delays, and sends the pulse signals to the mechanical drive system to control stepper motors to complete the final engraving.

Based on a survey of the development and performance requirements of engraving machines both domestically and internationally, the design requirements for the CNC engraving machine control system in this paper are as follows:

1.  Maximum travel for X-axis, Y-axis, and Z-axis (spindle) are 300, 400, and 50 (mm), respectively;
2.  Frame material: 6061-T6 aluminum alloy;
3.  Spindle power: 1.5KW electric spindle, with a maximum speed of 24000 rpm/min;
4.  Transmission unit: TB11605 ball screw
