8-channel Vibration/Voltage, Low-Frequency, Low-Noise, High-Gain (24-bit)

Data Acquisition Terminal Board

![](https://pub-048dcb96257f476697b113fcb5939cb9.r2.dev/blog/144676889/01_08a2d27f8e524aea851cfe9c4b034f6e.png)

![](https://pub-048dcb96257f476697b113fcb5939cb9.r2.dev/blog/144676889/02_6f72dbb767549408e865902bd7cbda3.png)

Featuring a hardware architecture based on the domestic AG16KF256 FPGA and STM32F429, it is primarily designed for low-frequency, low-noise application scenarios. The maximum sampling rate is 1 Ksps, and the minimum sampling rate is 50 sps. Under conditions of maximum sampling rate and maximum gain, the noise is merely 35 nVp-p, making it highly suitable for acquiring low-frequency small signals.

**(II) 16-channel Vibration/Voltage/Current + 16-channel Temperature (24-bit) High-Precision Data Acquisition Terminal Board**

![](https://pub-048dcb96257f476697b113fcb5939cb9.r2.dev/blog/144676889/03_4b82563c93e041dc801087199245e583.png)

![](https://pub-048dcb96257f476697b113fcb5939cb9.r2.dev/blog/144676889/04_41f8634765be47c8b8c11faca3a94a7d.png)

Utilizing Xilinx's ZYNQ7020 as the main control chip, each acquisition card features 16 channels. Each channel uses a 7-pin LEMO connector, with four pins serving as general-purpose input interfaces. Onboard relay switching allows these to be configured into IEPE vibration, voltage, or current modes. The other three pins are dedicated PT100 input interfaces, allowing connection of 3-wire PT100 sensors for temperature acquisition. The entire acquisition card incorporates surge protection, common-mode filtering, differential-mode filtering, and isolated power input, providing excellent suppression of electromagnetic compatibility (EMC) interference.
