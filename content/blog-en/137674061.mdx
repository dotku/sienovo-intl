Concept of Intelligent Transportation:

Intelligent Transportation System (ITS) is the future direction of transportation systems. It effectively integrates advanced information technology, data communication transmission technology, electronic sensing technology, control technology, and computer technology, applying them to the entire ground transportation management system. This creates a real-time, accurate, and efficient integrated transportation management system that operates comprehensively and across a wide range.

Intelligent Transportation Systems improve transportation efficiency through the harmonious and close cooperation of people, vehicles, and roads, alleviating traffic congestion, enhancing road network capacity, reducing traffic accidents, lowering energy consumption, and mitigating environmental pollution.

According to ITS applications in a certain region, the predicted benefits for 2015 were: a 10% to 50% reduction in traffic congestion; a 5% to 15% saving in energy; over a 25% reduction in air pollution; a 5% to 25% reduction in enterprise operating costs; and a 30% to 60% reduction in accidents.

Advantages:

Intelligent Transportation Systems have the following two characteristics: First, they focus on the widespread application and services of traffic information. Second, they aim to improve the operational efficiency of existing transportation facilities. Compared to general technical systems, the requirements for integrity during the construction of Intelligent Transportation Systems are more stringent. This integrity is reflected in:

(1) Cross-industry characteristics. The construction of Intelligent Transportation Systems involves numerous industry sectors, making it a complex, large-scale system engineering project with broad societal participation, which leads to complex inter-industry coordination issues.

(2) Technical domain characteristics. Intelligent Transportation Systems integrate achievements from numerous scientific fields such as transportation engineering, information engineering, control engineering, communication technology, and computer technology, requiring collaborative efforts from technical personnel across many domains.

(3) Joint participation of government, enterprises, research institutions, and universities. Appropriate role definition and task allocation are crucial prerequisites for the effective implementation of the system.

(4) Intelligent Transportation Systems will primarily be supported by new-generation information technologies such as mobile communication, broadband networks, RFID, sensors, and cloud computing, better meeting human application needs, increasing trustworthiness, and becoming "ubiquitous."
