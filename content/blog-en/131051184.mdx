1. Video input, with visible light using LVDS and infrared using CameraLink.

2. H.264 low-bitrate compression encoding for local storage.

3. H.264 compression outputs real-time streams using a synchronous 422 interface or network port UDP protocol.

4. Uses serial port for communication, such as between visible light, infrared, laser, or ground control software.

5. Network connection for UDP-based communication control.

6. Integrates common algorithms for optoelectronic payloads:
        a. Target localization, target velocity and direction measurement
        b. Target tracking
        c. Moving target detection
        d. Electronic image stabilization
        e. Target detection
        f. ... ...

7. Servo control algorithms are planned for addition, using interfaces like SPI or serial port to access data from encoders, gyroscopes, etc., for servo control (to be completed in the next phase).

RK3588+FPGA High-Speed Image Processing and Communication Processor Solution.

(1) Based on RK3588 + Xilinx Artix-7 FPGA processors;
(2) The RK3588 EVB is equipped with the RK3588 chip, manufactured with an 8nm process. It features a quad-core ARM Cortex-A76 + quad-core ARM Cortex-A55 CPU, a built-in 6 TOPS independent NPU, and utilizes the RK806-2 dual PMIC power supply solution. It supports LPDDR4X/eMMC storage and has over 10 peripheral interfaces such as HDMI, MIPI, VGA, and SATA. It boasts high integration, strong performance, and excellent expandability, meeting the product requirements of various AIoT terminal devices across industries.
(3) The RK3588 and FPGA are connected via communication interfaces such as uPP, EMIF, I2C, PCIe, and SRIO, with PCIe and SRIO each supporting a maximum transmission speed of 5 GBaud per lane;
(4) The FPGA acquisition card supports dual-channel 250 MSPS * 12-bit high-speed, high-precision ADCs, and a single-channel 175 MSPS * 12-bit high-speed, high-precision DAC, meeting various data acquisition requirements;
(5) Supports Gigabit Ethernet port for connecting industrial network cameras, and also supports common interfaces such as I2C, SPI, UART, and McBSP;
(6) Supports expansion modules such as CameraLink input/output and VGA output;

Recently, Rockchip released two development platforms based on its flagship chip RK3588: the RK3588 EVB and RK3588S EVB. The former is suitable for AIoT industry applications such as ARM PCs, NVRs, servers, IPCs, and large-screen display devices, while the latter is suitable for high-end tablets, AR/VR, personal mobile internet devices, and other products.

The RK3588 EVB is equipped with the RK3588 chip, manufactured with an 8nm process. It features a quad-core ARM Cortex-A76 + quad-core ARM Cortex-A55 CPU, a built-in 6 TOPS independent NPU, and utilizes the RK806-2 dual PMIC power supply solution. It supports LPDDR4X/eMMC storage and has over 10 peripheral interfaces such as HDMI, MIPI, VGA, and SATA. It boasts high integration, strong performance, and excellent expandability, meeting the product requirements of various AIoT terminal devices across industries.

![](https://pub-048dcb96257f476697b113fcb5939cb9.r2.dev/blog/131051184/06_1a89519067b3a2ea564b739c2cc46903.jpeg)

The RK3588S EVB is equipped with the RK3588S chip. Targeting different application markets, it has streamlined some interfaces, making it suitable for consumer electronics products such as high-end tablets, AR/VR, and personal mobile internet devices that require thinness and portability.
