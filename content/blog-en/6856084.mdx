Anti-Mac Interface (GUI vs. CLI)  
  
"Visual interfaces work well when handling a small number of objects with simple behaviors. However, as the complexity or number of objects increases, direct manipulation quickly turns into tedious, repetitive labor. The drawback of a directly manipulatable interface is that everything must be operated manually. In contrast to an executor issuing high-level commands, the user is reduced to an assembler, forced to repeatedly perform the same tasks over and over."
