An anesthesia depth monitor is used to monitor the depth of anesthesia during surgery. It can accurately reflect the patient's anesthetic state and sedation level during the surgical period, allowing for real-time monitoring of the patient's anesthesia depth.

The core board is based on NXP's i.MX8M Mini quad-core 64-bit processor, clocked at 1.8GHz, and features an ARM Cortex-A53 architecture. It also supports a general-purpose Cortex-M4 400MHz core processor. It provides various peripheral interfaces and high-definition display capabilities, which, combined with the high-performance processor, ensure smoother operation of the monitoring device.
