The number of GMSL cameras that can be connected to RK3588 depends on the deserializer board model and configuration scheme used:

1.  ‌**xcDeserializer3.0 Deserializer Board**‌  
    Can connect up to ‌**8-channel 2M GMSL2 cameras**‌1.
    
2.  ‌**xcDeserializer4.0 Deserializer Board**‌  
    Supports ‌**4-channel 2M GMSL2 cameras**‌1.
    
3.  ‌**Edge Computing Box Solution**‌  
    Some commercial solutions can enable ‌**4 or 8 channels of GMSL camera input**‌, for example, NVR solutions adapted for RK3588 support multi-channel intelligent detection38.
    

![](https://pub-048dcb96257f476697b113fcb5939cb9.r2.dev/blog/147953359/01_1c56bf9cc8094c2b8e228de7d03248d9.jpeg)

‌**Hardware Connectivity Support:**‌

*   RK3588 itself supports ‌**6 MIPI interfaces**‌ (via DCPHY or CSI modules)46, requiring a deserializer board to convert GMSL signals to MIPI signals before connecting to the development board.
*   In terms of software configuration, the Kernel needs to be upgraded to support the deserializer board, and the camera started via example programs (such as `xcCamera`)1.

‌**Summary:**‌  
Theoretically, RK3588 can achieve higher channel GMSL camera input through combinations of multiple deserializer boards, but in actual projects, a comprehensive design is required based on the deserializer board model, ISP allocation, and software compatibility.
