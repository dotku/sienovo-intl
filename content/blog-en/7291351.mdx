1. Alternate activities. If you code in the morning, then in the afternoon you can: go to the lab for debugging, attend meetings, discuss with colleagues, find a suitable place to listen to appropriate music for 5-10 minutes, do some exercises like horse stance, or even take a 5-minute stroll downstairs.
It's crucial for your brain to switch activities. You shouldn't continuously think about the same problem for too long (more than 4 hours).
In fact, the most important thing is to prepare several different types of problems in the morning, then solve one in the morning and one in the afternoon – that's perfect.
The benefit of discussion is that it gives software engineers a chance to speak, and discussing a problem with another person makes it more interesting.
