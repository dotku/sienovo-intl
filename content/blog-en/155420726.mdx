Royole flexible display X86 and ARM display solutions exhibit significant differences in architecture compatibility, performance, and application scenarios:

#### X86 Architecture Solutions

*   **Performance Advantages**: Suitable for high-load scenarios such as multi-system access and complex data processing, capable of maintaining smooth display refresh rates‌1.
*   **Compatibility**: Supports traditional industrial software and full operating systems, with a wide range of driver compatibility‌16.
*   **Thermal Design**: Requires enhanced thermal structures to handle high power consumption, suitable for high-temperature environments‌1.

![](https://pub-048dcb96257f476697b113fcb5939cb9.r2.dev/blog/155420726/01_abedaa06ade44eb4bd99e49fded99200.png)

#### ARM Architecture Solutions

*   **Low Power Consumption**: Lightweight design with low heat generation, suitable for embedded installations and quiet environments‌12.
*   **Flexible Deployment**: Supports Android/Linux systems, suitable for lightweight applications and edge computing‌23.
*   **Dual-Screen Asynchronous Display**: Cortex-A8 and above chips can easily achieve asynchronous multi-screen display (e.g., for advertising machines, medical terminals)‌3.

#### Selection Recommendations

*   **Heavy-Load Scenarios** (e.g., industrial control, real-time monitoring): Prioritize X86 to ensure performance and stability‌16.
*   **Lightweight Applications** (e.g., information inquiry, basic interaction): ARM is more energy-efficient and flexible to deploy‌23.
*   **Multi-Screen Requirements**: ARM solutions are more cost-effective and suitable for asynchronous display scenarios‌3.
