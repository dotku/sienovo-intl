**Digital Medical Devices: Fully Automatic Urine Sediment Analyzer**

The digitization of medical devices, as it is called, is a new modern medical approach that leverages computer technology and integrates advanced techniques like big data analytics and artificial intelligence recognition. It automates processes such as data acquisition, storage, transmission, and processing, enabling dynamic visualization of data and real-time sharing of analysis results. For instance, the advent of fully automatic urine sediment analyzers, as highly intelligent and fully automated instruments for analyzing formed elements in urine, can significantly enhance the efficiency, precision, and accuracy of tests, thereby reducing the burden on medical professionals.

**Embedded Computers Applied to Urine Sediment Analyzers**

Fully automatic urine sediment analyzers have reportedly become mainstream equipment for routine urinalysis in clinical laboratories, nephrology labs, and similar settings. They primarily consist of a urine sediment main unit, a microscope, a CCD image acquisition system, a computer control system, a display terminal, and corresponding urine sediment recognition and analysis software. The urine sediment main unit introduces the sample into a flow cell placed on the microscope stage. The fully automatic microscope then automatically searches, focuses, acquires urine sediment images, and transmits them to the computer. Relevant analysis software automatically identifies formed elements in the urine sediment, generating graphical data displayed on the monitor. Finally, after review by a laboratory physician, a complete urine analysis report can be generated.

During the operation of the fully automatic urine sediment analyzer, all operations are automatically completed with a single click under computer control, requiring no manual intervention in the analysis process. Therefore, to ensure the instrument's automatic and efficient operation and the accuracy of analysis results, high-quality, high-performance computer hardware is essential to provide foundational support.
