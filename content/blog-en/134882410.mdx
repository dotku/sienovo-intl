During marine vessel operations, a vibration data acquisition system is typically required to monitor the performance of various mechanical and electrical equipment. This enables rapid early warning, diagnosis, analysis, and maintenance when equipment failures occur, ensuring the normal operation of marine mechanical and electrical systems, thereby safeguarding both crew members and the vessel itself.

Various mechanical and electrical systems on board generate significant noise. When equipment malfunctions, these issues may be masked by ambient noise, making them difficult to detect even during manual inspections. Time-consuming processes, high error rates, and poor timeliness further increase the risk of undetected faults. By leveraging industrial intelligent gateways, automatic data collection and fault alarms can be achieved, enabling quick identification and resolution of problems, thus optimizing operational efficiency.

![](https://pub-048dcb96257f476697b113fcb5939cb9.r2.dev/blog/134882410/01_1e1873296d2153bc6bac3ce53a64a3cf.png)

By connecting vibration sensors to the Wattnet Industrial Intelligent Gateway, vibration data can be collected in real time and instantly uploaded to a local host computer or cloud platform for monitoring. Users can view parameter changes at any time and receive alarm notifications, facilitating prompt response and control measures.

System Functions

1. Data Acquisition and Networking: The gateway can simultaneously collect data from multiple vibration sensors and supports connectivity via 5G/4G/Wi-Fi/Ethernet, allowing flexible configuration based on site conditions.

2. Fault Alarm Notifications: When abnormal vibrations indicating equipment faults occur, the system automatically triggers alarms and sends notifications to on-site large displays, smartphones, and computers, showing the faulty device, time of failure, and detailed fault information.

3. Data Report and Analysis: The system allows users to access real-time and historical data at any time. Through data reports, operational efficiency of mechanical and electrical equipment can be analyzed and optimized, improving overall equipment management efficiency.
