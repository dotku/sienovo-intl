The Human-Machine Interface (HMI) is an interactive display device used for interactive information display for drivers and crew. It can also be used for CCTV monitoring. While supporting connections for various interface devices, its embedded Linux system can meet customers' customized requirements.

Key Features
Touch buttons are located around the display area, offering excellent durability with no mechanical wear and tear, and are easy to configure as required. It can be customized as a full touchscreen, without touch control buttons around the display area.

Built-in ultra-low-power quad-core processor, with a processing capability of up to 1.2 billion instructions per second, configured with a 2D/3D graphics and video acceleration processor engine, and an independent multimedia processor engine.

Equipped with 2 high-speed RS232 serial ports and 2 opto-isolated high-speed RS232/RS485 selectable serial ports, optional 2 high-speed isolated CAN bus interfaces, 1 10M/100M/1000M adaptive network port, and 2 high-speed USB2.0 ports.

Rail Transit Prognostics and Health Management (PHM) Unit

![](https://pub-048dcb96257f476697b113fcb5939cb9.r2.dev/blog/146249018/02_d7178d9d274841359b80d7d856c8bd8.png)

The Traction Motor Prognostics and Health Management (PHM) Unit is used for monitoring the bearing status and fault prediction of traction motors. Through composite sensors (temperature + vibration), it collects vibration acceleration and temperature data of traction motor bearings, receives speed signals, and by combining the traction motor PHM on-board unit hardware with its embedded software, it achieves automatic data acquisition, data processing, storage, feature extraction, fault early warning, and data transmission for traction motor bearing data.
