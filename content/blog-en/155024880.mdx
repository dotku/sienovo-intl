## FPGA-based NVMe Storage Array Expansion Card: A Cost-Effective Solution for Data Centers

In the pursuit of reducing data center storage costs, footprint, and power consumption, edge-AI and industrial computing companies are continually exploring innovative solutions. One such approach is the use of FPGA-based NVMe storage array expansion cards, which offer a reliable and high-throughput way to transmit NVMe frames. In this article, we will delve into the concept of directly connected accelerators and NVM-over-Fabric, and explore how this technology can be used to create remote, tiered storage or Ethernet Just a Bunch of Flash (EJBOF).

### Success Stories

FPGA-based NVMe storage array expansion cards have emerged as an ideal choice for NVMe storage arrays due to their ability to provide low latency and high throughput. By leveraging this technology, data centers can significantly reduce storage costs, footprint, and power consumption. The use of directly connected accelerators and NVM-over-Fabric enables the reliable transmission of NVMe frames, making it an attractive solution for remote, tiered storage or EJBOF applications.

### Product Specifications

The FPGA-based NVMe storage array expansion card features two 100GbE ports via two QSFP28, providing high-speed connectivity and scalability. The onboard DDR4 SDRAM ensures efficient memory access and processing, while the ZU19EG MPSoC (Micro-Blaze processor) provides the necessary processing power for complex computations.

### Technical Details

The use of directly connected accelerators and NVM-over-Fabric in FPGA-based NVMe storage array expansion cards enables the creation of remote, tiered storage or EJBOF. This technology relies on the low latency and high throughput of NVMe frames to transmit data efficiently. By leveraging this approach, data centers can reduce storage costs, footprint, and power consumption, making it an attractive solution for edge-AI and industrial computing applications.

By leveraging the benefits of FPGA-based NVMe storage array expansion cards, data centers can improve their storage efficiency, reduce costs, and enhance their overall performance.
