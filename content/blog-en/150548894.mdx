Professionally designed for energy management scenarios in energy storage and microgrids, this solution integrates Ethernet ports, CAN, RS485, RS232, and DI/DO. It fully meets the data acquisition and I/O control requirements for BMS, PCS, diesel generators, and PV inverters. Meanwhile, the software layer integrates standard acquisition protocols and is compatible with interfaces from various mainstream equipment manufacturers, making it widely applicable in industrial and commercial energy storage, residential energy storage, and microgrid control scenarios.![](https://pub-048dcb96257f476697b113fcb5939cb9.r2.dev/blog/150548894/01_7f5ff301534f46d49f861c80ade2fd04.png)

Scenario Introduction

The EMU Energy Management and Monitoring Controller is an innovative technology with outstanding advantages, specifically tailored for energy storage and microgrid scenarios. Compared to multiple traditional devices such as 4G routers, 5G routers, and PLC controllers, the EMU enables integrated management, simplifying system architecture, reducing the number of devices, and lowering maintenance costs. At the same time, it provides efficient energy management functions, optimizing energy storage and utilization through real-time monitoring and intelligent control. This controller can precisely control energy storage equipment and offers flexible energy dispatch strategies to improve energy utilization efficiency and enhance system stability and reliability. Whether for industrial, commercial, or residential users, the EMU Energy Management and Monitoring Controller provides convenience and benefits for their energy needs, making it an ideal solution for energy storage system operation.

Product Introduction

It adopts a high-end ARM processor, a quad-core ARM Cortex-A55, with integrated GPU and NPU. It offers advantages such as high efficiency, high performance, and low cost, supporting 2-4GB RAM and 32GB onboard storage. The gateway integrates functional interfaces such as Ethernet, 4G, 5G, WiFi, RS485, RS232, HDMI, and DI/DO.
