Smart cockpit systems include Driver Monitoring System (DMS) and Occupant Monitoring System (OMS). By installing cameras inside the vehicle, these systems perceive driver and passenger behavior as well as in-cabin conditions. DMS cameras enable functions such as driver fatigue monitoring, driver attention monitoring, dangerous driving behavior detection, and driver identification. OMS cameras can detect occupant-related data within the cockpit to further enhance safety. The in-cabin behavior recognition task aims to identify the behaviors of drivers and passengers within the vehicle cockpit, providing technical support to improve the driving and riding experience, thereby offering drivers and passengers a better experience and safer travel.

![](https://pub-048dcb96257f476697b113fcb5939cb9.r2.dev/blog/140032841/01_937b972cb9ee424eb3af63a92d5a156e.png)

Our smart cockpit cameras utilize high-dynamic range (HDR) automotive-grade image sensors, paired with mainstream serial transmission chips. They feature global shutter and 940nm infrared illumination. Notably, the OMS cameras employ RGBIR technology, capable of simultaneously capturing RGB color images and IR images, thus providing day and night visibility. This offers all-weather, high-quality image data support for visual perception technology within smart cockpit systems.
