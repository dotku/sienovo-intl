A fully automatic blood cell analyzer, also known clinically as a CBC analyzer, blood analyzer, or blood cell counter, is a routine clinical laboratory instrument that automatically analyzes the heterogeneity of blood cells within a certain volume of whole blood.

The NXP IMX6Q core board features a quad-core Cortex-A9 architecture, clocked at 1GHz, and utilizes a 12-layer PCB immersion gold process to ensure optimal electrical performance. It supports multiple display interfaces to accommodate various display screens. Additionally, the CPU includes 3D acceleration and hardware video encoding/decoding, ensuring smooth graphics rendering and video playback for system applications.
