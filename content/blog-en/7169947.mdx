Proof: According to the Pigeonhole Principle, consider n+2 positive integers. Construct n+1 pigeonholes based on their residue classes modulo 2n: {0, 2n}, {1, 2n-1}, {2, 2n-2}, ..., {n-1, n+1}, {n}. Therefore, at least two numbers must fall into the same pigeonhole, so their sum or difference must be divisible by 2n.
