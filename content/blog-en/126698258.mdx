Machine vision is a branch of computer vision. Visual control or visual applications in the field of industrial automation primarily refer to extracting relevant information from digitized images captured by front-end optical cameras. After analysis and processing, this information is used for controlling production line processes or quality, thereby replacing repetitive manual operations. Currently, practical applications of machine vision in factory production include: integrity quality inspection of workpieces, identification, classification, part position and orientation, dimensional measurement, code reading and traceability, surface/color difference detection, automated assembly line operations, and so on. It can be said that machine vision equips assembly lines and automated equipment with a pair of "eyes," capable of providing round-the-clock, highly consistent, and highly reliable inspection results, enhancing the intelligence level of factory production, and thus becoming an important technology for advancing towards intelligent manufacturing.
