“**Orthogonality**” is a term borrowed from geometry. If two lines intersect at a right angle, they are orthogonal, like the coordinate axes in the diagram. In vector terms, these two lines are independent of each other. Moving along one line does not change your position projected onto the other line.

In computing, the term is used to denote a kind of independence or decoupling. If a change in one of two or more things does not affect the others, those things are orthogonal. In a well-designed system, database code and the user interface are orthogonal: you can change the interface without affecting the database, and replace the database without changing the interface.
