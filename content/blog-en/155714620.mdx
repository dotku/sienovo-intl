Scenario Requirements: Embodied AI faces challenges in dynamic and complex real-world environments, simultaneously achieving "precise understanding of 3D space," "real-time correlation with body movements," and "flexible adaptation to scene changes." Currently, mainstream vision solutions are distributed in the robot's head and wrist. Head vision requires global perception to achieve environment modeling, object recognition, and interaction understanding; while wrist vision requires close-range fine perception to support grasping and positioning, operational precision control, and object state judgment. The two work together to meet the demands of global decision-making and local fine manipulation.

■ Equipped with an industrial-grade image sensor, offering stronger environmental adaptability and anti-interference capabilities, stably outputting high-quality images;
■ Built-in IMU module to enhance motion vision accuracy;
■ Adopts 2-in-1 GMSL transmission technology for more efficient and convenient integration.
