Unmanned forklifts and machine vision are two distinct technological domains, yet they can be integrated to achieve more efficient logistics automation. Unmanned forklifts are automated transport vehicles capable of moving and transporting goods without human intervention. Machine vision, an artificial intelligence technology, enables computers to recognize and interpret content within images or videos. In logistics automation, machine vision assists unmanned forklifts in identifying cargo, obstacles, and surroundings, thereby enabling better path planning and collision avoidance.

![图片](https://pub-048dcb96257f476697b113fcb5939cb9.r2.dev/blog/136463814/01_b613c701ba821f1fcdf68b83916a8dc4.png)

A dedicated controller for unmanned forklifts and machine vision acts as the central nervous system, playing a decisive role in this process. A high-performance, stable controller skillfully coordinates the movement of unmanned forklifts while ensuring accurate and error-free execution of commands. On December 15th at 10:00 AM, we are launching a new product — the Dedicated Controller for Unmanned Forklifts & Machine Vision — to drive the efficient development of smart logistics.

 **New Product Highlight** 

This is a high-performance industrial box PC based on the H610 chipset, powered by Intel® 12th Gen Alder Lake-S and 13th Gen Raptor Lake-S series processors. Featuring complete I/O functionality and a compact form factor, it is ideal for applications in unmanned forklifts, machine vision, industrial control, and related fields.
