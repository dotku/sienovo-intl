## I. Introduction

As system complexity continues to increase, data processing techniques for single-sensor systems can no longer meet requirements. To compensate for the limitations of single sensors, multi-sensor information fusion technology has emerged. Infrared and visible light image fusion is one of the important research directions in the field of multi-source image fusion.

## II. UHD Fusion Camera Solution and Hardware Design

### 
2.1 Camera Function Requirements

The UHD fusion camera system designed in this paper is capable of real-time acquisition of visible and infrared light image information, performing real-time fusion and H.265 encoding of the acquired image information, and finally transmitting it via the RTP protocol. The system also supports encoding and transmission of multiple video streams. The specific functional requirements of the system are as follows:

(1) Camera Mode Configuration:
Parameters such as image sensor resolution, exposure time, and frame rate can be configured via low-speed serial interfaces like SPI, IIC, and UART, adapting to changes in actual acquisition scenarios.

(2) Image Acquisition Function:
The system can receive and process pixel data output from visible and infrared light sensors in real time.

(3) Image Fusion Function:
Real-time fusion of acquired visible and infrared light images is performed using image fusion algorithms.

(4) Video Stream Encoding and Transmission Function:
The system integrates H.264 and H.265
