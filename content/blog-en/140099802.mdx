Application Scenarios

Smart warehousing is a segment of the logistics process. The application of smart warehousing ensures the speed and accuracy of data input at various stages of goods and warehouse management, allowing enterprises to promptly and accurately grasp real-time inventory data, and to reasonably maintain and control their inventory. Currently, the handling process largely employs AGVs. AGV intelligent handling robots automatically transport items to designated locations using special landmark navigation. The most common guidance methods include magnetic strip guidance, laser guidance, and RFID guidance.

##### Client Requirements

Ultra-thin, low power consumption, fanless, capable of supporting operating temperatures from -10~60°C, and requiring high shock resistance. Supports bus interfaces such as USB, COM, and LAN. Additionally, it supports expansion functions like 4G/Wifi/LoRa, ensures 24/7 uninterrupted operation, and supports wide voltage input and display output.

![](https://pub-048dcb96257f476697b113fcb5939cb9.r2.dev/blog/140099802/02_b9711b519da05150978d4ef95d3aefb5.png)

### Product Features

Onboard Intel® Celeron® J1900 high-performance, low-power quad-core processor, with a maximum clock speed of 2.0GHz
Supports single-channel DDR3L 1333 SO-DIMM memory slot, with a maximum memory capacity of 8GB
Supports dual Gigabit Ethernet ports
Supports multi-display expansion via VGA/HDMI
Built-in 1 x Mini-PCIe and 1 x M.2 for various module expansions
9~36V wide voltage design, with lockable DC-IN connector
