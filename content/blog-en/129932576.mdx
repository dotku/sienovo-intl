My personal views (without involving any project-related details or trade secrets, these are merely my general observations):

1. From a macro trend perspective, the flexibility and versatility of [humanoid robots](https://www.zhihu.com/search?q=%E4%BA%BA%E5%BD%A2%E6%9C%BA%E5%99%A8%E4%BA%BA&search_source=Entity&hybrid_search_source=Entity&hybrid_search_extra=%7B%22sourceType%22%3A%22answer%22%2C%22sourceId%22%3A2963630653%7D "humanoid robots") are indeed very high, and they hold great promise. Moreover, wheeled-legged locomotion is not unique to this approach; ETH Zurich's ANYMAL quadruped/wheeled robot is an excellent example, demonstrating excellent mobility and flexibility. It can be widely applied in various industrial and commercial scenarios. 【**In short, if it can be successfully developed, it will be highly valuable in terms of both versatility and practicality.】**

2. However, legged robots, especially [bipedal robots](https://www.zhihu.com/search?q=%E5%8F%8C%E8%B6%B3%E6%9C%BA%E5%99%A8%E4%BA%BA&search_source=Entity&hybrid_search_source=Entity&hybrid_search_extra=%7B%22sourceType%22%3A%22answer%22%2C%22sourceId%22%3A2963630653%7D "bipedal robots"), still face unresolved challenges in motion control within academia. There's a long way to go for applications involving more complex scenarios. It's not just about the fundamental [control theory](https://www.zhihu.com/search?q=%E6%8E%A7%E5%88%B6%E7%90%86%E8%AE%BA%E5%9F%BA%E7%A1%80&search_source=Entity&hybrid_search_source=Entity&hybrid_search_extra=%7B%22sourceType%22%3A%22answer%22%2C%22sourceId%22%3A2963630653%7D "control theory"); more importantly, it involves refining the product and adapting it to various scenarios, which requires significant investment of capital and effort. Of course, as a major KOL, he has access to funding channels, and I privately have a general understanding that he has the capability to embark on this endeavor, though it remains a long and arduous journey. As someone with the capability and determination to pursue this, we should remain hopeful and wait to see what unfolds. A potentially significant challenge here is the motors; they are difficult to develop, expensive to produce in-house, and off-the-shelf options might not perform optimally. Furthermore, implementing legged/wheeled transitions is not easily achieved with [motion control algorithms](https://www.zhihu.com/search?q=%E8%BF%90%E5%8A%A8%E6%8E%A7%E5%88%B6%E7%AE%97%E6%B3%95&search_source=Entity&hybrid_search_source=Entity&hybrid_search_extra=%7B%22sourceType%22%3A%22answer%22%2C%22sourceId%22%3A2963630653%7D "motion control algorithms"). 【**In summary, this project presents significant technical challenges, requiring substantial investment of capital and effort to develop algorithms and refine the product.】**
