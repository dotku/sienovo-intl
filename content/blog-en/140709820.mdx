It is widely recognized that medical imaging equipment is one of the most promising sub-sectors in the medical device industry. CT scanners (referred to as CT), for example, are an ideal choice for doctors in clinical examination and diagnosis of various diseases.

**Development Potential and Challenges of Medical CT Scanners**

CT, or Computed Tomography, uses a computer to control and adjust X-ray beams, ultrasound, etc. High-sensitivity detectors scan the human body layer by layer from all angles to generate digital signals, which are then integrated on a computer platform to produce a detailed image of the human body. It features fast scanning times, comprehensive data, and clear images, capable of revealing minute lesions in organs, abnormalities in bone density, and more. This assists doctors in more efficient and accurate lesion diagnosis and treatment planning, leading to its widespread use.

Currently, CT occupies the largest sub-market within China's medical imaging equipment market. According to an article published by DianShi Capital, in 2020, CT sales in China exceeded 7,200 units, and in 2021, they surpassed 7,000 units. The total number of CT units sold in these two years exceeded the combined sales of the previous five years (2014-2019). In the future, with China's economic development, the exacerbation of population aging, and the continuous growth in citizens' demand for medical services, the market size for CT will further expand.

However, medical imaging equipment faces high technical barriers and high manufacturing costs. Furthermore, key technologies and core components have long been monopolized by foreign companies. The development of domestic CT and other medical imaging equipment also faces challenges such as low industry concentration and a scarcity of computer hardware products on the market that meet their demands for high resolution, high sensitivity, high stability, and specialized service. In response, Xinmai has actively strategized in the medical imaging equipment sector, continuously deepening communication and cooperation with industry clients, and has developed embedded computer products that support various medical imaging equipment applications, including CT scanners.
