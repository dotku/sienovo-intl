Digital Multimeter (DMM)

• 6½-digit display

• 24-bit resolution

• 5 S/s - 250 kS/s sampling rate

• Both power supply and digital I/O utilize isolated noise immunity technology

• High-precision measurement of voltage, current, resistance, inductance, and capacitance

• Diode/Transistor test

### Power Analyzer

*   Adjustable output voltage from 0.8V to 14V, with a maximum continuous output current of 8A;
*   Real-time acquisition of voltage, current, and power data, with graphical display via software;
*   Supports data acquisition from external power supply devices for external device power analysis;
*   Multiple devices can be used simultaneously for parallel monitoring;
*   Supports both USB and network communication methods, as well as local data storage functionality;
*   Features power-on self-test and multi-faceted protection, including: over-voltage, under-voltage, over-current, and over-temperature protection.

![](https://pub-048dcb96257f476697b113fcb5939cb9.r2.dev/blog/139552164/01_8c35fa4f5799848d459ccb9f35991d2f.png)

The power analyzer is primarily used for power supply and power analysis of 3C electronic products, and can monitor the product's voltage, current, and power changes in real-time via host computer software. Design and development engineers can use this instrument to analyze and optimize mobile device performance, while maintenance and testing personnel can use it to detect problems in devices.
