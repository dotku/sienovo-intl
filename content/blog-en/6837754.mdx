[Foreword]
This article, upon its publication, garnered significant attention, which was to be expected. After all, C, C++, and the creator of Linux are all very familiar topics to everyone. However, many students focusing their energy on pure debates about language superiority and inferiority isn't very meaningful. One of the protagonists in this debate, Microsoft's Dmitry Kakurin, made a very accurate statement: "It's a matter of faith. Therefore, any reasoning and debate will be endless and meaningless..."

I believe the correct attitude should be to draw insights from the discussions of experts, enhance and deepen one's understanding of relevant languages, and consider how to learn and use them in one's own practical context.
