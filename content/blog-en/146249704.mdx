The hardware system of the Traction Control Unit (TCU) is a multi-processor-based embedded system. This system fully integrates the powerful computational and logical processing capabilities of Digital Signal Processors (DSP), embedded processors (PowerPC, ARM), embedded microcontrollers (MCU), and FPGAs. Combined with a real-time operating system, a single TCU can achieve control of up to 4 sets of rectification/inversion. Its configuration can also be flexibly adjusted according to actual product applications. The architecture of a typical configuration is as follows:

![qianyin1.jpg](https://pub-048dcb96257f476697b113fcb5939cb9.r2.dev/blog/146249704/02_6f80dcab6d5788e4f1ffc8bc20d32d58.jpeg)

Key Features:
▶ Independent hardware configuration for rectification control and inversion control (DSP + ADI + PIO);
▶ CPCI bus – for high-speed communication between MCPU, NETI, DSP, and OUTI boards;
▶ CAN bus – for communication between I/O interface boards (including DI, DO, and AIO boards) and NETI boards;
▶ Ethernet, RS485, CAN, MVB, and other bus interfaces – for external communication of the Traction Control Unit;
▶ The system adopts an open architecture, featuring high integration, compact structure, and strong scalability;
▶ Designed based on standard buses, allowing for free combination of boards;
▶ The operating system has high real-time performance, and user programs have good portability;
▶ The main control board and DSP have strong floating-point computing capabilities, meeting current control and computation needs, and reserving capacity for future functional expansion.
▶ Equipped with comprehensive self-detection and fault diagnosis functions, offering good fault diagnosis and localization, and strong maintainability;
▶ Features automatic hardware board recognition, capable of automatically loading corresponding drivers or software based on board information;
▶ Includes safety protection measures such as overcurrent, overvoltage, undervoltage, overspeed, feedback protection, and power-off protection, effectively preventing further spread of system failures;
▶ Provides functions such as process monitoring, process recording, and fault recording, facilitating process and fault analysis.
