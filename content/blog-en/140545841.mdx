**Applications of Embedded Computers in Immunoassay Equipment**

Immunoassay equipment is a type of in vitro diagnostic instrument that is increasingly applied in various clinical fields, meeting the needs of the developing medical testing industry. Fully automatic electrochemiluminescence immunoassay (ECLIA) analyzers are currently one of the most widely used immunoassay devices.

**Application Requirements for Fully Automatic Electrochemiluminescence Immunoassay Analyzers**

Fully automatic electrochemiluminescence immunoassay analyzers are sample-selective benchtop analyzers designed for medium to large laboratories or testing centers to perform heterogeneous immunoassays. They combine luminescence reactions with immune reactions, utilizing micro-amplification technology, etc. Compared to other immunoassay equipment, they **offer higher detection sensitivity, a wider range of detectable analytes (from traditional proteins, hormones, and enzymes to drugs, etc.), and shorter reaction times**, which can effectively improve testing quality and work efficiency.

It is understood that the system design of fully automatic electrochemiluminescence immunoassay analyzers mainly consists of three unit modules: the computer control system, the electrochemical detection system, and the sample tray and reagent kit. According to testing requirements, their main performance and hardware requirements are as follows:

**Accuracy**

Requires relevant hardware to have powerful computing and storage capabilities.

The instrument performs automatic data processing and uses 2D barcode technology for safe reagent tracking. All data information generated during operation needs to be analyzed and processed by the computer control system. Therefore, the relevant hardware must have excellent computing power and support high-speed storage.

**Reliability**

Requires relevant hardware to be secure, trustworthy, stable, and reliable.

According to testing requirements, fully automatic electrochemiluminescence immunoassay analyzers support various rapid tests for different practical applications, and the machine must be readily available. This requires the device's hardware platform to have a low-power design, high environmental anti-interference capability, and a long equipment lifecycle.

**Intelligence**

Requires relevant hardware to have strong expandability and be easy to operate.

Fully automatic electrochemiluminescence immunoassay analyzers feature programmed sample loading. The computer control system is a critical part of the instrument and serves as its command and control center. It performs functions such as programmed operation, automatic monitoring, indication judgment, and data processing. Therefore, the relevant hardware must have rich interfaces and strong expandability to meet the instrument's multi-functional extension and development.
